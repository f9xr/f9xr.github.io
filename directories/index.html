Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about product launch directories.

Product launch directories are online platforms where founders and startups can submit their products, SaaS tools, or apps to gain visibility, attract early users, and earn SEO backlinks. Examples include Product Hunt, BetaList, and Hacker News.
They help you acquire your first users, build social proof, earn high-quality backlinks for SEO, and get feedback from early adopters. Many successful startups credit their initial traction to a well-executed directory launch.
Most product launch directories offer free listings or a free tier. Some have paid options for additional visibility, featured spots, or expedited reviews. Our curated list highlights free and affordable options to get you started.
Results vary depending on the platform and your product quality. Some directories approve listings within hours, while others may take several days. You can expect initial traffic and backlink signals to appear within the first two to four weeks after submission.
Yes, absolutely. In fact, submitting to multiple directories is recommended for maximum visibility and backlink diversity. Each platform reaches a different audience, and appearing on multiple trusted sites strengthens your domain authority and search engine rankings over time.
The best directories for SaaS products in 2026 include Product Hunt, BetaList, G2, Capterra, SaaSHub, AlternativeTo, Uneed, Hacker News, Peerlist, and MicroLaunch. Each platform targets different audiences - Product Hunt for early adopters, G2 and Capterra for enterprise buyers, and BetaList for startup enthusiasts. A combination of these gives your SaaS maximum visibility across the entire customer journey.
Getting listed on most directories is free and straightforward. Start by creating an account on each platform, then follow their submission guidelines to add your product or business details. Ensure your NAP (Name, Address, Phone) is consistent across all listings for local directories. For product launches, prepare compelling descriptions, screenshots, and a demo video.
